Divine Mysticism

Distinguished from Diabolical Counterfeits and Human Analogies — one of the most thorough systematic treatments of Catholic mystical theology ever written, in four volumes.

Translated from the French · Paris · Librairie Ch. Poussielgue, 1895. English rendering 2026 · the original is in the public domain.

Volume I Contemplation

The nature, degrees, causes, and effects of mystical prayer — the soul’s ascent toward union with God.

1895 Volume II The Mystical Phenomena

Visions, locutions, revelations, and the extraordinary graces that accompany the mystical life.

1895 Volume III The Diabolical Counterfeits

Discernment of spirits — the marks by which divine mysticism is distinguished from its counterfeits.

1895 Volume IV The Human Analogies

The natural counterparts of mystical experience — the phenomena of body and soul that resemble, without attaining, the divine.

1895
About this edition. Canon J. Ribet’s La Mystique Divine is one of the most thorough systematic treatments of Catholic mystical theology ever written. This edition presents a complete machine-assisted draft translation of the 1895 Poussielgue edition in four volumes — a working draft, not a critical edition.
